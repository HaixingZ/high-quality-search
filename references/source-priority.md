# Source Priority

Use this order unless the user gives stricter instructions.

## Priority Ladder

1. `official PDF`
   Direct PDF hosted by the publisher, issuer, regulator, exchange, company, association, or authorized event page.
2. `official page`
   Official landing page with the report, slide deck, transcript, or download button, even if the PDF is absent.
3. `authorized distribution`
   Publicly accessible databases, event microsites, or partner pages that clearly identify the original publisher and the document.
4. `secondary mirror`
   Reposts, aggregators, or file hosts. Use only when the original cannot be found publicly. Label the risk.
5. `commentary`
   News stories, blogs, decks quoting the report, or SEO summaries. Use only for discovery or context, not as the primary deliverable.

## Preferred Source Classes

### 1. Consulting and professional-services research

Prioritize named research centers, insights pages, and downloadable reports from the original firm.

Typical targets:
- McKinsey
- BCG
- Bain
- Deloitte
- PwC
- EY
- Kearney
- Oliver Wyman
- Roland Berger
- Accenture
- Capgemini

### 2. Regulators, exchanges, and public bodies

Use these when the user needs market structure, policy, compliance, or official statistics.

Typical targets:
- securities regulators
- stock exchanges
- ministries and government agencies
- multilateral institutions
- central banks

### 3. Company investor-relations and annual publications

Use these for strategy, market sizing clues, product roadmaps, annual reports, investor decks, capital-markets-day decks, and sustainability reports.

Typical targets:
- investor-relations sites
- annual report portals
- earnings presentation libraries
- investor-day pages

### 4. Standards bodies, trade associations, and industry consortia

Use these for technical white papers, standards, benchmark frameworks, and industry-wide adoption data.

Typical targets:
- standards organizations
- trade groups
- conference proceedings with downloadable papers
- association research centers

### 5. Broker and bank research

Treat this category carefully because availability varies by license.

Prefer, in order:
1. Public research, outlooks, strategy notes, and thematic reports posted on the institution's official site.
2. Public conference decks, sector primers, annual outlook books, and macro strategy PDFs from official domains.
3. Licensed portals or client-only pages, clearly labeled `licensed/paywalled`.
4. Unofficial reposts only as a last resort, clearly labeled `secondary mirror` and never described as licensed or official.

When the user asks for broker reports:
- search official institution pages first
- keep original title, analyst or desk name if available, and date
- state access limits explicitly
- prefer metadata plus official page over a dubious mirror

## Ranking Rules

Score higher when a source is:
- closer to the original publisher
- newer within the relevant window
- directly about the requested topic rather than adjacent
- downloadable as a PDF or slide deck
- clearly dated and attributable

Score lower when a source is:
- undated
- heavily summarized by a third party
- duplicated across mirrors without provenance
- optimized for SEO rather than publication quality

## Tie-Breakers

When two sources look similar, prefer:
1. original host over repost
2. PDF over HTML summary
3. named authoring institution over generic portal
4. precise publication date over vague year-only mention
5. broad flagship report over a thin derivative summary
