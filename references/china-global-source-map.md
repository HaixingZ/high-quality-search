# China and Global Source Map

Use this file to shortlist likely official publishers before broad web search.

## China

### Regulators and public bodies

Start here when the topic involves policy, market rules, industrial planning, standards, or sector supervision.

Common source classes:
- ministries and commissions
- industry regulators
- statistical agencies
- local-government development zones and policy offices
- state-backed think tanks and research institutes

Useful document intents:
- `白皮书`
- `发展规划`
- `行动方案`
- `征求意见稿`
- `统计公报`
- `年度报告`
- `行业运行情况`

### Exchanges and market infrastructure

Use for listing rules, consultation papers, market statistics, sector handbooks, and market structure material.

Common source classes:
- mainland stock exchanges
- futures exchanges
- clearing houses
- industry self-regulatory bodies

Useful document intents:
- `规则`
- `问答`
- `市场统计`
- `业务指南`
- `研究报告`

### Company and issuer channels

Use for investor decks, annual reports, ESG reports, bond-offering documents, prospectuses, and capital-markets-day material.

Common source classes:
- investor-relations pages
- annual-report libraries
- exchange filing pages
- bond market disclosure portals

Useful document intents:
- `年报`
- `半年报`
- `投资者演示`
- `路演材料`
- `招股书`
- `募集说明书`

### Chinese research ecosystems

Use for broader industry material that may not sit on government or company sites.

Common source classes:
- industry associations
- standards alliances
- conference organizers with downloadable proceedings
- university or lab white-paper centers
- consulting and accounting firms' China insight portals

## Search Order

When the user has not named institutions, use this default order:
1. regulators or public bodies
2. exchanges or market infrastructure
3. company IR or issuer disclosure pages
4. top consulting or accounting firms
5. associations and standards groups
6. multilateral institutions
7. broker and bank public research hubs

## Practical Rule

If a topic looks commercial but evidence quality is weak, switch one level closer to the source:
- from media summary to official issuer page
- from aggregator PDF to publisher landing page
- from reposted report title to the originating institution's insight center
